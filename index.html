Shalom + Aloha

God’s design for and delight in creativity and diversity are embedded in everything we see: order, peace, relationships, stewardship, love, beauty, and rhythm. These are the essential foundations for Shaloha, “the way God designed the universe to be.” Both the people of Israel and the people of Hawaii have used these words, Shalom and Aloha, powerfully, as a way to greet, bless, and direct others in the direction of God's original design. May we pursue and cultivate shaloha.

About the Cause

The diet of the modern Hawaiian culture is not the same as the Hawaiians of old, who grew all their own food. Today, food is shipped from overseas. In return for packaged and processed food, Kauai provides tourism, entertainment, a few fruits, and GMO testing grounds. Kauai Underground Artist (KUGA) aims to serve Kauai with farming jobs and healthy, fresh food.

By developing a small-scale agriculture model that works, KUGA seeks to expand to a large scale on agriculture land in west Kauai and restore what Akua (God) had planned for the Garden Island. Along with this, the greater goal is to provide a positive environment for youth and others to be in a peaceful place, learn many skills, and grow in God's love.
